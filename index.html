A little bit about me

My name is Anusha Dharmasena. I am a 31-year-old Software Engineer, originally from Sri Lanka, and I've been developing software for over 8 years. I moved to Australia at the beginning of 2016 with my family. We live in a South Eastern suburb of Melbourne.

I love lots of things: programming, blogging, learning, reading about new tech in the world of PCs and playing PC games to name a few. Nowadays I spend my leisure time learning new web development because I want to move in that direction.

I havea a son and a daughter and they are very young; they are not even going to kinder yet. That's my son with me in the photo, which was shot at the Brighton beach on a lovely evening.

Career at a glance

First couple of years - Sri Lanka

After finishing university, I joined a software company in Sri Lanka which had its HQ in Japan. For the first couple of years, I worked on a few web projects with technologies that would be considered very old in today's world. After that, I was tranferred to its HQ in Japan to work on the good stuff.

Next five and a half years - Japan

I spent about five and a half years in Japan, where I mainly developed printer driver related products for Canon Inc.

Printer drivers?

That does not sound exciting at all!

Yes, but I learned a lot of things when I was in Japan.

I did not work at Canon exactly; Canon simply outsourced many of their projects to the company I worked for. However, my final year in Japan, I was deployed to one of Canon's technical centres to work on the Windows V4 Printer Driver. We managed to bring it up to a level which could replace the aging V3 driver much earlier than the management anticipated, which was a huge victory for the team.

The most interesting project that I was part of when I was in Japan was a protytype product for a concept Canon was bringing to the market. I would call it "The SVG project". Sadly, it was a very secretive project, so I cannot speak much about it in public. What we built was a PDF to SVG converter, but there were almost impossible requirements. To name a few, the resulting SVG file had to be indistinguishable from the original PDF document pixel-to-pixel when rendered, be smaller in payload and render faster in a web browser! None of the tools available in the market could do this. By now, that conceppt could probably be a reality and perhaps I can talk about it; however there is no way to know as it was not targeted at the general consumer. It was my first Agile project, and I was a main designer/developer of the team, right from its inception.

First year after moving to Australia

Soon after I moved to Melbourne, I found a job through a referral. Despite being hired as an Embedded Software Engineer, I was asigned to developing a React.js web app, the purpose I probably should not talk about in public.

I learned React.js stuff while on-the-job as well as during my leisure; and I still do. The deeper I dug into React.js, the more I fell in love with it. It's official: I want to move into web development full time. This is also what made me want to design this website. But this is just a hobby project and will - hopefully - constantly evolve.
