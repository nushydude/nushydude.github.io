Career

In Sri Lanka

After finishing university, I joined a software firm with its HQ in Japan. I learned Japanese and worked on Web Applications during my 2 year span there.

In Japan

Next I got the opportunity to work at the HQ in Tokyo. Initially I was assigned to printer driver projects, which were in their maintenance phases.

After three years, I got the chance to develop a new product for Canon. Internally it was called "The SVG project". The goal was to build a PDF to SVG converter - conforming to very, very strict regulations. It was my first Agile project. The phase 1 was a success, however I was pulled out of phase 2 to work on the V4 project which was in a critical condition.

The goal of the Windows V4 Printer Driver project was to port features of the to-be-phased-out V3 driver. Microsoft had deadline in place, and it needed a lot of work to meet that. Our team was so young, passionate and agile, that we could bring it on track for release in only 9 months.

In Australia

After moving to Australia, my experience landed me a job quickly. However, these days I am working on a completely different field. I am developing a web product. It's a React.js web UI for a smart embedded comtroller.
